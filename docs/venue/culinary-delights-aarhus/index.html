Culinary Delights in Aarhus

Aarhus boasts a vibrant and diverse dining scene, offering something for every palate. Whether you’re a gourmet aficionado or simply in search of a satisfying meal, the city has plenty to explore. For a casual start, head to the Latin Quarter or Jægergårdsgade, where you’ll find a wide range of dining options.

Fine Dining

For those seeking fine dining, Aarhus is home to several Michelin-starred restaurants, which you can explore. The city is also featured in the White Guide Denmark, showcasing some of the country’s top dining spots.

Street Food

If street food is more your style, don’t miss Aarhus Street Food near the bus station, where over 30 stalls serve a variety of international dishes. Another great option is Nicolinehus Market Hall in Aarhus Ø, offering global flavors like dumplings, falafel, and oysters.

The Visit Aarhus Guide

Looking for something in between gourmet and street food or just looking for something..? Check the VisitAarhus guide for a wide selection of cafés and restaurants in the Aarhus region, including Restaurant Havnær, the venue for the CHR 2024 conference dinner.

Coffee and Baked Goods

For coffee and baked goods, Aarhus offers a wealth of delightful spots. La Cabra Coffee and David Breadhead are known for their artisanal brews and fresh pastries. Other must-visit bakeries include Schweizer Bageriet, Berta, Gotfred, Mor Anna, and Jumbo. For more information on coffee shops, take a look here.
